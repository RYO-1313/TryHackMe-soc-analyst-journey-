## 🔍 What the room covered

An introduction to the SOC Level 1 Analyst role — the team structure of a Security Operations Center, the daily responsibilities of a Junior Analyst, and how different roles collaborate during security incidents of varying severity.

---

## 🧠 What I actually learned

Most of the SOC structure and Junior Analyst responsibilities aligned with prior knowledge. The one concept that was formally clarified in this room was the **escalation path**: a Junior Analyst does not independently handle complex cases. After completing initial triage and analysis of an alert, the correct action is to escalate to the **Senior Analyst** — not to attempt resolution independently. This distinction between detection, initial analysis, and escalation is a core operational boundary for the L1 role.

---

## 💡 Key concept to remember

**The L1 escalation path:**  
Alert triggered → L1 monitors and performs initial analysis → escalates to Senior Analyst → if major incident, Incident Responder is engaged

Each tier has a defined scope. Staying within that scope is not a limitation — it is how a SOC functions efficiently and without gaps.

---

## 🔑 How this connects to the SOC Analyst role

Understanding where the L1 role begins and ends is operationally critical. Overstepping by attempting to handle complex cases independently — or understepping by escalating prematurely without initial analysis — both create risk for the organization. This room reinforced that effective SOC work is as much about **process discipline** as it is about technical skill.
